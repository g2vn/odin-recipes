Fettuccine Al Burro


Back to Home

Description

Fettuccine Al Burro is a staple, but did you know it's not exactly italian? In any case, this simple dish is easy to make, quick to prepare, and so delicious!

With a short list of ingredients, a small prep time, and an amazing taste, this dish is one I prepare for myself and my family often. Can't beat tasty and cheap.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (16 ounces) fettuccine pasta
  • 3/4 cup (about 1 1/2 sticks) salted butter (a personal preference, but unsalted is best...)
  • Kosher (or Sea) Salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • Grated parmigiano...HEY! I SAID PARMIGIANO, NOT PARMESAN...trust me, the difference is BIG.

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta by bringing a large pot of water to a rolling boil.
    • Rule of thumb, 4 quarts of water per pound of pasta, scale as needed
    • Keep in mind, the water does NOT need to actively be boiling to cook it, hence the term rolling!
    • When cooked, reserve 1 cup of the pasta water and drain in a colander. Do NOT rinse the pasta.

  2. With the drained Fettuccine, return to the pot and add the butter and 1/4 cup of the reserved pasta water.
    • Add a pinch of your chosen salt, and half of your parmigiano. This will begin to emulsify and create a light sauce.
    • Using a pair of tongs, toss the pasta, adding more pasta water and parmigiano as needed. The sauce will become thicker and cheesier!

  3. Serve immediately and top with leftover parmigiano, black pepper and other garnishes.

There you have it, an incredible serving of Fettuccine Al Burro. I hope you enjoy!
